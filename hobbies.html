Problem Writing

I have written problems for many mock contests in the past few years. Most prominently, I have written for the Christmas Math Competitions on the Art of Problem Solving website, as well as for the nationwide NEMO math contest (I also serve here as a logistics manager).

Here are some of my favorites, roughly in order of ascending difficulty.

  • Five real numbers are selected independently and at random from the interval $ (0,1) $. Let $ m $ be the minimum of the five selected numbers. What is the probability that $ \frac{1}{4} < m < \frac{1}{2} $?
  • What is the largest positive integer $ n $ such that $ n! $ divides $ \frac{(5!)!}{(4!)!} $?
  • The four unique positive integer solutions $ (a,b) $ to the equation $ 25^2 + 128^2 = a^2 + b^2 $ are $ (25,128),(128,25),(m,n), $ and $ (n,m) $ for positive integers $ m,n \neq 25,128 $. Find $ mn $.
  • Regular hexagon $ ABCDEF $ has side length 1. Equilateral triangle $ XYZ $ is drawn such that $ A $ is on $ \overline{XY} $, $ C $ is on $ \overline{YZ} $, and $ E $ is on $ \overline{ZX} $. If $ 70\% $ of the interior of $ ABCDEF $ is also withn the interior of $ XYZ $, find $ XY $.
  • Let $ t = 2017^{2017} $. The value of \[ \frac{1}{\pi} \sum\limits_{n=2} ^{t} \text{cos} ^{-1} \left( \frac{1 + n\sqrt{n^2 - 4}}{n^2 - 1} \right), \] where all calculations are done in radians, is a real number $ A $. Find the integer closest to $ 1000A $. Note: The notation $ \text{cos} ^{-1} x $ implies that the principal value be taken; in other words, for all $ x $, $ \text{cos} ^{-1} x \in [0,\pi] $.
